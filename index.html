About

Carrier charges are designed to go uncontested

Demurrage and detention invoices stack up in accounts payable — complex, jargon-heavy, and easy to overlook. Carriers count on importers paying without question. We make sure they don't.

01

You send the invoice

Forward any demurrage or detention invoice to our team. We handle Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM, Hapag-Lloyd, ONE, OOCL — every major carrier operating into Australian ports.

02

We build the dispute

Cross-reference against carrier tariffs, terminal gate logs, holiday calendars, and free-time entitlements. Every contestable charge documented with carrier-specific evidence. 48-hour turnaround.

03

You recover the charges

Dispute filed directly with the carrier. When approved, the reversal lands on your account. You pay 30% of what we recover — and nothing if we don't win.

04

Specialists, not generalists

A small Sydney-based team focused exclusively on demurrage and detention disputes. No account managers, no enterprise onboarding — just specialists who know carrier tariff rules and Australian port operations.

What We Cover

Demurrage and detention dispute recovery

Demurrage disputes

Gate-out timestamp verification against terminal records. Free-time recalculation for public holidays and port closures. Carrier-specific tariff citation and direct filing through claims portals. Average recovery: $500–5,000 per dispute.

Detention disputes

Return-date verification against terminal check-in data. Force majeure and port shutdown waivers. Equipment availability delay claims. Carrier-caused detention arguments backed by terminal operating system timestamps.
