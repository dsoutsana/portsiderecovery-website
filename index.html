The Problem

Carrier charges are designed to go uncontested

Demurrage and detention invoices stack up in AP departments. They're complex, jargon-heavy, and most importers lack the resources to audit every line.

01

Stacking invoices

A single delayed container generates multiple invoices from terminal operators, shipping lines, and transport providers. Most go uncontested because nobody has time to cross-reference them all.

02

Hidden free time

Carriers offer free days that don't match your actual gate-out window. Public holidays, port congestion, and terminal closures extend your entitlement — but the carrier won't volunteer that information.

03

Tariff complexity

Every carrier — Maersk, MSC, Hapag-Lloyd, CMA CGM — has its own tariff rules, dispute portals, and evidence requirements. Your logistics team shouldn't need to learn all of them.

04

No internal bandwidth

Your logistics team is busy keeping freight moving. Disputing charges requires carrier-specific evidence that takes hours per invoice to compile — hours your team doesn't have.

What We Cover

Specialist dispute recovery

Primary

Demurrage disputes

We audit demurrage charges for gate-out discrepancies, free-time miscalculations, public holiday extensions, port congestion waivers, and terminal closure exceptions. Average recovery: $500–5,000 per dispute.

Secondary

Detention disputes

Detention charges accrue when containers aren't returned on time — often due to factors outside your control. We identify chargeable vs. non-chargeable detention days and dispute the difference.
